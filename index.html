Bamboo Fiber Extractor

Prototype Development.

Gradient Engineering was working on developing processes to extract bamboo fiber from raw cane. The goal of this project was to develop a prototype machine that could brush and straighten the fibers. The initial prototype utilized a drum brusher which was later changed to an electrically powered belt style brusher. Gradient uses this machine to process and straighten bamboo fibers for use in composite materials.

  • Date: November 2013-May 2014
  • Client: Gradient Engineering
  • Category: Process development

Material Characterization

Materials Research.

Daniel Guest participated in materials research for Gradient Engineering to analyze several key parameters of bamboo fiber materials. One of the more notable studies was aimed at understanding the effects of several coupling treatments on the bamboo fiber. Daniel utilized Montana State University's Scanning Electron Microscope (SEM) as well as water contact angle measurements to determine the surface energy of the treated material. Gradient was ultimately provided with a thorough analysis of the treatments.

  • Date: November 2013-May 2014
  • Client: Gradient Engineering
  • Category: Material Analysis

Aerial Sensor Platforms

Prototype Design.

Anasphere hired Daniel to work on the Phase I and II development of a novel atmospheric icing sensor. Initially the design utilized a single wing samara airfoil, based on an efficient bioinspired design, to carry the instrument payload. Daniel researched the design, modeled the flight characteristics and ultimately fabricated a full scale prototype for testing. In Phase II development a more stable helicopter platform was developed and prototyped. Daniel was also instrumental in develoing novel sensors for detecting ice.

  • Date: August 2013-February 2016
  • Client: Anasphere Inc.
  • Category: Prototype Development

36' Closed Loop Icing Wind Tunnel

Design and Manufacture.

One of the challenges of Anasphere's Phase II project with NASA was to simulate cloud icing conditions in a lab setting. Daniel was asked to design and build a wind tunnel that was capable of reaching and maintaining sub-freezing temperatures at airspeeds of up to 10 meters per second. Research was conducted on documented wind tunnels and ultimately a design was developed. Daniel fabricated the wind tunnel and designed and assembled all the electrical controls for the fan drive and the fluid delivery plumbing for a misting spray system.

  • Date: May 2014-December 2014
  • Client: Anasphere Inc.
  • Category: Design and Manufacturing

TetherSonde Weather Station

Product Redesign.

Anasphere's TetherSonde line of products was in need of an upgrade and Daniel was brought in to design a new electronics housing and tether components. The basic design requirements were a smaller and lighter electronics housing and improved line attachment design. SolidWorks was used to model components that were later manufactured using both CNC milling as well as plastic injection molding. The newly redesigned TetherSonde system is simpler to manufacture and easier for customers to operate.

  • Date: June 2014-December 2014
  • Client: Anasphere Inc.
  • Category: Plastic Ijection Molding, Design for Manufacturing

RAK-IT Analysis

Strength Design Analysis.

The owners of RAK-IT had to make an informed decision about materials for their newly invented utility stand. Utilizing FEA analysis tools TrueSource was able to help them to realize cost and weight savings over traditional materials.

  • Date: September 2016
  • Client: RAK-IT
  • Category: FEA Analysis
